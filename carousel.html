Iterations

Glass, steel

2020

Iterations is the culmination of a series of works on self-harm. Through the process of slumping and kiln-forming, nine glass panels were created using the same mold, which deteriorated with each firing. The resulting ethereal shifts across panels emphasize the importance of discerning: both looking at the object and looking through, physically and metaphorically. The engraved words of my story are barely visible, just as scars of the body are not necessarily on the surface and viewable to all. This folding screen acts as a second skin, a permeable surface holding memory, just as glass never forgets its history.

Slumber Party

Interactive installation

4’x4’x5’

April 2019

This piece acts as a celebration of the relation- ship between mothers and daughters; how love can exist between family members despite the hardships they give one another. Utilizing the act of knitting as a unifying factor in the artist’s re- lationship with his own mother, the artist invites others to learn how to knit in his blanket fort and catalyzes discussion into the intersection into the public and the private.

Untitled (A Study of Failure)

Video projection

4’x5’

April 2019

Exploring the idea of the seen and unseen within glassblowing and the hotshop. Often times all that is seen by those who do not blow themselves is the beautiful outcome, not the thousands of failures it took to get there. Utilizing the Pepper’s Ghost technique the artist creates a projected illu- sion of himself working (and failing) in the empty shop.

Sa-BLOW-Tage!

Game prototype: 1 instruction sheet, 30 varied ves- sel cards, 30 blank cards

3.5”x3.5”x4.5”

April 2019

“Sa-Blow-Tage!” is a game to be played in the hotshop. All skill levels are welcome The objec- tive of the game is to be the first team to create your chosen glass object in the hotshop —but there’s a catch. Each team will be working from the same blowpipe/and or punty. The team that creates a vessel most closely resembling their card wins!

Studies in Mimicry: Glassphalt

Glass, asphalt

3.5”x2.5”x.8”

March 2019

Utilizing alginate the artist created a negative of a piece of asphalt. Utilizing the alginate the art- ist created a faux pate de verre style mold. The fused frit greatly mimicked the look and texture of the actual asphalt. By displaying it among real pieces of asphalt, finding the "Glassphalt" almost becomes a game.

Core and Shell Studies

Glass, apples, 3 video stills, 8 process photos

October 2018

An apple was used as a mold to create glass objects reflecting the shape of the apple’s core. The mold slowly burnt away as more glass filled it. This process was repeated until the mold was unable to be used. Contrasting this process, thin glass was wrapped around an apple core to cre- ate a new outer shell.
